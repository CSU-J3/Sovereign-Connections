## Lead 1 — Vulcan Elements / 1789 Capital → Connected Procurement (not FSF)

**Disposition: route to Connected Procurement. Out of scope for FSF.**

**Corrected facts (video was wrong on two; primary-tier source is ProPublica, not the video):**

- Company is **Vulcan Elements** (North Carolina rare-earth magnet startup), not "Vulcan Earth."
- The instrument is a **$620M Pentagon loan**, not a contract.
- Trump Jr.'s VC firm **1789 Capital** took a stake of **undisclosed size** ~3 months before the Pentagon announced the loan. The video's "$200M → $2B valuation" framing is uncorroborated against the ProPublica account, which says only that valuation estimates rose roughly tenfold post-announcement.
- ProPublica's reporting (May 28, 2026) attributes the loan request to White House adviser Peter Navarro, and is framed as the first time a federal award was directly tied to White House intervention for a Trump-linked firm. Pentagon, Vulcan, and Trump Jr. all denied his involvement.

**Why it's out of FSF scope:** no foreign sovereign or sovereign-adjacent capital in the documented chain. The money is a US Pentagon loan; the rare-earth program is a China-supply-chain-reduction effort; the financial interest is a domestic VC firm. It fails the sovereign-source test the same way SC-006 (Burisma) does, just from the domestic side. FSF's spine is sovereign-fund-to-family-business flows; this is family-business-to-federal-money, which is a different beat.

**Why it's a strong Connected Procurement candidate:** named family member (Trump Jr. via 1789 Capital), financial interest (undisclosed VC stake), government action (Pentagon loan), documented timing overlap (~3 months), and a documented intervention mechanism (Navarro request, per ProPublica). That's most of a procurement record's spine.

**To promote (in Connected Procurement, not here):**
- Pull the ProPublica piece as the category-3 anchor (https://www.propublica.org/article/donald-trump-jr-vulcan-deal-white-house) and trace its cited Defense Department documents to category-1 primary records.
- Confirm 1789 Capital's stake via any filing that puts a number or date on it; the size is currently undisclosed, so the financial-interest field may stay a documented-but-unquantified entry.
- Watch the second lead ProPublica names: a drone-parts manufacturer Trump Jr. advises and holds a stake in, reportedly also under Pentagon lending review. Separate record if it firms up.

## Lead 2 — Iran $300B investment fund → FSF watch item (pre-candidate)

**Disposition: FSF watch item. Not a candidate. No primary source exists yet.**

**What the video actually claims:** that the US side *floated* a ~$300B Iran investment fund as part of a long-term deal, sourced to "Clash Report," with Kushner and Witkoff said to personally profit. The host explicitly says he doubts it comes to pass.

**Why it's only a watch item:** the entire claim is a secondary-source report of a *proposed* idea, hedged by the speaker, with no transaction, no filing, no closed deal. Under the tracker's primary-source verification discipline, a floated proposal relayed by a partisan aggregator doesn't clear the bar for a candidate, let alone a record. There is nothing to parse and nothing to promote. Logging it prevents re-triage and marks what would change its status.

**Why it's FSF-shaped if it ever firms up:** if a fund with Iranian state or state-adjacent capital reaches a documented stage and family entities hold an interest, it's the same structure SC-007 already tracks (envoy-negotiated deal, family/Witkoff equity exposure) and SC-001 anchors comparatively (Affinity/PIF). The convergent-interest flag from v2.0 would apply directly.

**What would move it from watch item to candidate:**
- A primary or near-primary source establishing the fund exists as more than a floated idea (a term sheet, an OGE filing reflecting an interest, a corporate registration, a government readout — not an aggregator's "one idea floated was…").
- A documented Iranian sovereign or sovereign-adjacent capital source. Note this is itself a `sovereign_entities.json` question: Iran has no listed SWF in the current registry, and whether an Iranian state-investment vehicle clears the sovereign-and-sovereign-adjacent standard is undecided. That definitional call should happen *before* any record, not retrofitted to fit a transaction.

**Do not** build a record off the video, the "Clash Report" relay, or reparations-framing commentary. Wait for the primary stage.
