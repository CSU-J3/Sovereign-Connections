The actual PROJECT.md hierarchy has 5 tiers: (1) primary records, (2) tracker data with traceable primary record, (3) investigative reporting from named outlets that cites underlying documents, (4) named-expert analysis, (5) opinion. v2.0 draft now uses "category 1 sources or category 3 reporting that cites underlying primary documents" consistently.

## Voice/discipline reminders

PROJECT.md is strict: no em-dashes, no rhetorical-question closers, no manufactured "three takeaways," no "paradigm shift" / "unprecedented" / "shocking" / "staggering" / "alarming" language. Default to understatement. Analyst, not advocate. Running prose, not headers, unless the piece is genuinely multi-section. Cite specific case law, regulations, and filings when load-bearing.

User preferences add: no "delve," "underscore," "leverage," "robust," etc. No "It's not just X, it's Y" structures. No restating the question. No grand-statement openings or wrap-up closings. Bold-term-colon-explanation list patterns are the single most recognizable AI marker; avoid them.

Two drift patterns the project actively guards against: (a) "payment occurred" → "payment caused policy outcome" (causal drift); (b) "sovereign source paid" → "sovereign government paid" (the SWF-vs-government conflation). Cite the SWF's specific governance structure rather than treating it as identical to its government.
